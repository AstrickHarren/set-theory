Axioms of Set Theory

2.1 Relations

Definition 2.4 (Relation) A set \(R\) is a relation if all of its elements are ordered pairs1. Denote \(xRy\) as \((x, y) \in R\) for convinence. The sets \[ \dom R = \{ x: \exists y, (xRy) \} \\ \ran R = \{ y: \exists x, (xRy) \} \\ \] are called the domain and range of \(R\) resp. and they exist as subsets of \(\bigcup \bigcup R\).

The inverse of \(R\) is defined as \[ R^{-1} = \{ (x, y) : (y, x) \in R \} \] which exists as a subset of \(\ran R \times \dom R\). That is, \(xR^{-1}y \iff yRx\).
Definition 2.5 (Image) The image, and inverse image of \(A\) under \(R\) are defined as \[ R[A] = \{ y : \exists x \in A, xRy \} \\ R^{-1}[A] = \{ x : \exists y \in A, xRy \} \]
Theorem 2.2 \(R^{-1}[A] = (R^{-1})[A]\).
Proof. \[\begin{align*} x \in R^{-1}[A] &\iff \exists y \in A, && xRy \\ &\iff \exists y \in A, && y(R^{-1})x \\ &\iff &&x \in (R^{-1})[A] \end{align*}\]
Definition 2.6 (Composite) The composition of two binary relations \(S, R\) is defined as \[ S \circ R = \{ (x, z) : \exists y, (xRy \vee ySz) \} \] which exists as a subset of \(\dom R \times \ran S\). Immediately, \[ \dom (R \circ S) = S^{-1}[\dom R] \]
Proof. \[\begin{align*} x \in \dom (R \circ S) &\iff \exists z, && x(R \circ S)z \\ &\iff \exists y, z, && xSy \vee yRz \\ &\iff \exists y, && xSy \vee y \in \dom R \\ &\iff && x \in S^{-1}[\dom R] \end{align*}\]

  1. Notice this makes \(\emptyset\) also a relation↩︎
